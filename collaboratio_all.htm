Traffic Management Capability Maturity Model

Collaboration Guidance - Level 1 to Level 2

Why is Collaboration important?

The development and implementation of TSM&O requires a collaborative approach. The effectiveness of most strategies is dependent on improving the coordinated performance of each partner.

Improvement Target

From: Relationships and collaboration between stakeholder organizations are informal and ad hoc.
To: Collaboration with stakeholders is more formal and related to specific traffic management needs and projects.

Key Sub-dimensions

  • Operational Agency Collaboration
  • Local Government/MPO/RPTA Cooperation
  • Private-Sector Participation
  • Data Sharing
  • Research/Applied Research
  • Interaction with Public

Operational Agency Collaboration Action Plan (Level 1 to Level 2)

Sub-Dimension Summary

Actions under this sub-dimension focus on the relationships between the different operating agencies in the region, emphasizing the evolution from relationships based on personal connections to ones that are more formalized and well formed. Actions here help agencies develop more effective partnerships for day-to-day operations regionally.

Key Actions

  1. Create a list of conditions/events where collaboration is mutually beneficial and where collaborating agencies are willing to change/adapt.
  2. Establish shared data feed requirements, including security, formats, archiving, etc.
  3. Hold regular agency meetings to establish the groundwork for shared facilities, personnel, and resources.
  4. Develop agreements, architecture, and infrastructure to prepare for shared control.

Action: Create a list of conditions/events where collaboration is mutually beneficial and where collaborating agencies are willing to change/adapt.

Definition: Establishes and defines the initial conditions where collaboration is mutually beneficial among affected users.

Rationale: Starts agencies thinking about potential improvements and creating the culture for collaboration.

Responsible Party: State DOT.

Action: Establish shared data feed requirements, including security, formats, archiving, etc.

Definition: Constructs the details of shared data feeds.

Rationale: Establishes initial requirements to set up data requirements.

Responsible Party: State DOT, municipalities, MPO, first responders, law enforcement.

Action: Hold regular agency meetings to establish the groundwork for shared facilities, personnel, and resources.

Definition: Establishes a schedule of planned agency meetings.

Rationale: Creates environment to discuss potential improvements and address questions/concerns.

Responsible Party: State DOT, municipalities, MPO, first responders, law enforcement.

Action: Develop agreements, architecture, and infrastructure to prepare for shared control.

Definition: Develops agreements within local government to facilitate shared control.

Rationale: Establishes the groundwork for local government to share control with others.

Responsible Party: State DOT, municipalities, MPO, first responders, law enforcement.

Sub-Dimension Summary

Actions under this sub-dimension help an agency consider the best role for the private sector in traffic management. While a particular level or nature of private-sector participation is not specified, actions identified help agencies understand the needs and the potential of the private sector in their traffic management program.

Key Actions

  1. Develop limited business models involving private-sector partnerships for review and response by involved agencies and private-sector participants.
  2. Support participation and activities in state and national ITS America chapter.

Action: Develop limited business models involving private-sector partnerships for review and response by involved agencies and private-sector participants.

Definition: Establishes a limited level of private-sector involvement.

Rationale: Reveals partnership opportunities to meet current needs by investigation of private-sector involvement.

Responsible Party: State DOT, private-sector entities.

Action: Support participation and activities in state and national ITS America chapter.

Definition: Encourages employee participation in ITS America (or local chapter).

Rationale: Encourages dialogue with other agencies and private-sector partners by attending (or creating, if necessary) a state ITS chapter.

Responsible Party: State DOT, private-sector entities.

Data Sharing Action Plan (Level 1 to Level 2)

Sub-Dimension Summary

Actions under this sub-dimension help an agency transition from a myopic use of agency data to utilization of the wealth of data generated by and available to traffic management agencies. Actions in this area emphasize the exploration of alternative concepts, increasing the sophistication of data sharing programs and development of tools to utilize the expanded data sets in traffic management.

Key Actions

  1. Develop data sharing plan that identifies regional gaps in data and potential data sources to meet predetermined traffic management objectives.

Action: Develop data sharing plan that identifies regional gaps in data and potential data sources to meet predetermined traffic management objectives.

Definition: Initiates a data sharing plan that identifies gaps and data sources.

Rationale: Identifies gaps in regional data is essential to finding the right sources to meet the need.

Responsible Party: State DOT, municipalities, MPO, first responders, law enforcement.

Traffic Management Capability Maturity Model

Collaboration Guidance - Level 3 to Level 4

Why is Collaboration important?

The development and implementation of TSM&O requires a collaborative approach. The effectiveness of most strategies is dependent on improving the coordinated performance of each partner.

Improvement Target

From: Agencies collaborate on traffic management at a high level via engagement of regional stakeholders.
To: Agencies approach traffic management at the regional level, with robust regional collaboration and multimodal decision-making taking place across the entire region.

Key Sub-dimensions

  • Operational Agency Collaboration
  • Local Government/MPO/RPTA Cooperation
  • Private-Sector Participation
  • Data Sharing
  • Research/Applied Research
  • Interaction with Public

Operational Agency Collaboration Action Plan (Level 3 to Level 4)

Sub-Dimension Summary

Actions under this sub-dimension focus on the relationships between the different operating agencies in the region, emphasizing the evolution from relationships based on personal connections to ones that are more formalized and well formed. Actions here help agencies develop more effective partnerships for day-to-day operations regionally.

Key Actions

  1. Formalize the agreements (SOPs, etc.) once all agencies are comfortable with them.
  2. Identify remaining corridor programs and data feeds still operating in stand-alone mode that should be considered in a multi-agency sense.
  3. Facilitate peer-to-peer exchanges and engagements to gain competencies and exposure to previous experiences.

Action: Formalize the agreements (SOPs, etc.) once all agencies are comfortable with them.

Definition: Uses formalized collaboration agreements once all agencies are comfortable with them.

Rationale: Solidifies commitments and signal long-term viability of the concepts.

Responsible Party: State DOT, municipalities, MPO, first responders, law enforcement.

Action: Identify remaining corridor programs and data feeds still operating in stand-alone mode that should be considered in a multi-agency sense.

Definition: Identifies corridor programs and data feeds not yet part of a multi-agency partnership.

Rationale: Encourages the broadest view of services that might be considered for collaboration.

Responsible Party: State DOT.

Action: Facilitate peer-to-peer exchanges and engagements to gain competencies and exposure to previous experiences.

Definition: Encourages improved relationships among affected agencies.

Rationale: Brings decision-makers together to jointly explore inter-agency issues and solutions.

Responsible Party: State DOT, municipalities, MPO, first responders, law enforcement.

Action: Conduct joint training exercises to build relations and share resources.

Definition: Involves conducting training exercises with employees from multiple agencies, which encourages working side by side.

Rationale: Encourages partnering agencies and DOTs to work together on an off-line basis prior to critical need.

Responsible Party: State DOT, municipalities, MPO, first responders, law enforcement, transit.

Action: Jointly share control of ITS devices and other systems.

Definition: Involves sharing ITS components among multiple agencies.

Rationale: Supports effective use of resources by shared control of ITS components and associated priority schemes.

Responsible Party: State DOT, municipalities, MPO, first responders, law enforcement, transit.

Action: Share communications networks and devices.

Definition: Encourages joint and shared use of communication equipment.

Rationale: Moves beyond a jurisdictional basis requires consideration of bandwidth requirements and efficient equipment usage.

Responsible Party: State DOT, municipalities, MPO, first responders, law enforcement, transit.

Action: Undertake joint decision-making and operations.

Definition: Involves different levels of authority agreeing to share authority with peers from other agencies.

Rationale: Establishes lines of authority for conceivable scenarios.

Responsible Party: State DOT, municipalities, MPO, first responders, law enforcement, transit.
