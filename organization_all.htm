Traffic Management Capability Maturity Model

Organization and Workforce Guidance - Level 1 to Level 2

Why is Organization and Workforce important?

Efficient execution of processes supporting effective programs requires appropriate combination of coordinated organizational functions and technical qualified staff with clear management authority and accountability.

Improvement Target

From: In-house personnel have limited traffic management experience with no specialized experience in engineering, traffic analysis, etc. Agency personnel roles are fragmented.
To: Core staff knowledge, skills, and abilities (KSAs) are identified within the traffic management arena, and roles are linked across various responsible groups.

Key Sub-dimensions

  • Organizational Structure
  • Recruitment and Retention
  • Staff Development/Professional Capacity Building

Organizational Structure Action Plan (Level 1 to Level 2)

Sub-Dimension Summary

Actions under this sub-dimension help an agency create an organizational structure for traffic management that is effective. While there is no single recommended organizational structure, the actions emphasize clear identification of roles, responsibilities, leadership, and procedures necessary for the sustained growth of the program.

Key Actions

  1. Develop a comprehensive list of traffic management roles and responsibilities across the organization and identify cross-linkages to facilitate coordination.
  2. Provide list of cross-linked traffic management roles to all traffic management staff across the organization for reference and use in daily and strategic activities.
  3. Develop high-level training mechanisms for traffic management staff related to all critical traffic management functions within the agency.
  4. Schedule a regular offering of high-level training as a function of personnel turnover.

Action: Develop a comprehensive list of traffic management roles and responsibilities across the organization and identify cross-linkages to facilitate coordination.

Definition: Identifies and clarifies roles and responsibilities of all partners.

Rationale: Facilitates coordination between regional partners.

Responsible Party: State DOT, MPO, municipality, county.

Action: Provide list of cross-linked traffic management roles to all traffic management staff across the organization for reference and use in daily and strategic activities.

Definition: List the personnel assets and capabilities within of partner member agencies.

Rationale: Allows partner agencies to know who to call to receive support.

Responsible Party: State DOT, MPO, municipality, county.

Action: Develop high-level training mechanisms for traffic management staff related to all critical traffic management functions within the agency.

Definition: Establishes a program for ensuring that personnel assets are adequately cross-trained.

Rationale: Ensures that qualified personnel exist within agencies in the region.

Responsible Party: Individual agencies.

Action: Schedule a regular offering of high-level training as a function of personnel turnover.

Definition: Provides for regular training and capacity development for agency personnel assets.

Rationale: Ensures that there is a continuous supply of qualified personnel available to agency.

Responsible Party: State DOT, MPO.

Organizational Structure Action Plan (Level 2 to Level 3)

Sub-Dimension Summary

Actions under this sub-dimension help an agency create an organizational structure for traffic management that is effective. While there is no single recommended organizational structure, the actions emphasize clear identification of roles, responsibilities, leadership, and procedures necessary for the sustained growth of the program.

Key Actions

  1. Establish a core group for traffic management that includes staff for key roles requiring redundancy.
  2. Compile a database of traffic management personnel and resources across all organizational units and identify redundancy with core group roles.
  3. Create training courses/seminars that focus on critical aspects of all traffic management functions.
  4. Devise a training program that periodically cross-trains all staff on critical functions and is regularly updated as roles, responsibilities, duties, and/or functions change.
  5. Construct a basic hierarchy of backup staff for critical functions that can be handled by cross-trained staff as needed.

Rationale: Improves lines of communication and builds strong relationships with partner agencies.

Rationale: Improves the rapid identification of personnel assets available within an agency.

Responsible Party: State DOT, MPO.

Action: Create training courses/seminars that focus on critical aspects of all traffic management functions.

Definition: Ensures that agency assets are highly qualified to address mission of agency.

Rationale: Ensures that adequate personnel resources are available within the region to cover mission-critical activities and functions.

Definition: Creates a program for cross-training and professional development of in-house staff on mission-critical activities and functions.

Rationale: Ensures that multiple individuals have the capability to perform all mission-critical activities and functions.

Responsible Party: Individual agencies.

Recruitment and Retention Action Plan (Level 2 to Level 3)

Sub-Dimension Summary

Actions under this sub-dimension help an agency recruit and retain a workforce that is capable of meeting all the traffic management objectives. Actions emphasize cross-training and redundancy of capabilities in the workforce to minimize single points of failures in traffic management operations.

Key Actions

  1. Identify those staff ideal for succession into key traffic management positions.
  2. Create a clearly defined and consistently applied process for career advancement for traffic management staff.
  3. Establish a retraining program for staff to facilitate job reassignment for traffic management needs (e.g., systems engineering, modeling, software development/procurement, ITS hardware maintenance).

Rationale: Limits downtime associated with finding replacement personnel.

Responsible Party: Individual agencies.

Action: Create a clearly defined and consistently applied process for career advancement for traffic management staff.

Definition: Lists of training and experience needed for personnel to assume next level of responsibility within organization.

Rationale: Provides staff with incentive to remain within agency.

Responsible Party: Individual agencies.

Action: Establish a retraining program for staff to facilitate job reassignment for traffic management needs (e.g., systems engineering, modeling, software development/procurement, ITS hardware maintenance).

Definition: Ensures that staff members have the KSAs they need to progress within the organization.

Rationale: Ensures that staffing shortfalls can be filled quickly from within.

Responsible Party: Individual agencies.

Traffic Management Capability Maturity Model

Organization and Workforce Guidance - Level 3 to Level 4

Why is Organization and Workforce important?

Efficient execution of processes supporting effective programs requires appropriate combination of coordinated organizational functions and technical qualified staff with clear management authority and accountability.

Improvement Target

From: Traffic management staff members and their related knowledge, skills, and abilities are identified and established on a broad basis and within individual groups.
To: Traffic management program support exists to engage in advancement of knowledge, skills, and abilities. Formal and ongoing training on traffic management is offered and supported by the agency.

Key Sub-dimensions

  • Organizational Structure
  • Recruitment and Retention
  • Staff Development/Professional Capacity Building

Organizational Structure Action Plan (Level 3 to Level 4)

Sub-Dimension Summary

Actions under this sub-dimension help an agency create an organizational structure for traffic management that is effective. While there is no single recommended organizational structure, the actions emphasize clear identification of roles, responsibilities, leadership, and procedures necessary for the sustained growth of the program.

Key Actions

  1. Design a comprehensive plan that includes procedures for sharing all traffic management resources across all organizational units.
  2. Develop detailed training courses for all core functions that address all KSAs necessary to perform specific traffic management roles and responsibilities.
  3. Establish organizational hierarchy that supports and empowers traffic management decision-making at the level that requires it.

Action: Design a comprehensive plan that includes procedures for sharing all traffic management resources across all organizational units.

Definition: Ensures that all mission-critical functions and activities can be performed during times of significant staffing shortfalls via a strategic staffing and management plan.

Rationale: Ensures that adequate personnel resources are available within the region to cover mission-critical activities and functions.

Responsible Party: State DOT, MPO, municipality, county.

Action: Develop detailed training courses for all core functions that address all KSAs necessary to perform specific traffic management roles and responsibilities.

Definition: Constructs a program for cross-training and professional development of in-house staff on mission-critical activities and functions.

Rationale: Ensures that agency personnel have the best available tools to perform critical decision-making.

Responsible Party: Individual agencies, MPO.

Action: Establish organizational hierarchy that supports and empowers traffic management decision-making at the level that requires it.

Definition: Empowers critical personnel to make decisions necessary to succeed.

Rationale: Facilitates the decision-making process; reduces “paralysis by analysis.”

Responsible Party: Individual agencies.

Recruitment and Retention Action Plan (Level 3 to Level 4)

Sub-Dimension Summary

Actions under this sub-dimension help an agency recruit and retain a workforce that is capable of meeting all the traffic management objectives. Actions emphasize cross-training and redundancy of capabilities in the workforce to minimize single points of failures in traffic management operations.

Key Actions

  1. Establish a formal succession plan for traffic management positions to ensure capabilities are maintained.
  2. Structure a mentoring and training program to ensure that staff members slated for succession are prepared at the appropriate time.
  3. Implement procedures for capturing institutional knowledge for planned and unplanned personnel transitions.

Action: Establish a formal succession plan for traffic management positions to ensure capabilities are maintained.

Definition: Outlines the KSAs and experience needed to perform mission-critical tasks and activities as part of a strategic plan approach.

Rationale: Establishes a clear pathway for individuals to see advancement within the organization.

Responsible Party: Individual agencies.

Action: Structure a mentoring and training program to ensure that staff members slated for succession are prepared at the appropriate time.

Definition: Allows agencies to develop personnel assets and reduces OTJ training needed.

Rationale: Facilitates the retention of the best and the brightest within an agency.

Responsible Party: Individual agencies.

Action: Implement procedures for capturing institutional knowledge for planned and unplanned personnel transitions.

Definition: Formalizes a program for capturing and documenting reasons why particular functions or activities are performed.

Rationale: Ensures that institutional knowledge does not escape when personnel leave.

Responsible Party: Individual agencies.
